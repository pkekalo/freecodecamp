Arithmetic operations

ADDITION

The addition of two whole numbers is the total amount of those values combined.

Addition is written using the plus sign "+" between the terms; that is, in infix notation. The result is expressed with an equals "="sign.

Example: 6 + 2 = 8

Here are additional problems to understand aditional operation.

  • Peter has 3 appels and Maria gave 1 apple to Peter, how many apples Peter now have? (The Answer is 4).
  • On the road there were 4 red cars 3 yellow cars and 2 blue cars on the road, how many cars are on the road? (The Answer is 9)
SUBTRACTION

Subtraction is an arithmetic operation that represents the operation of removing objects from a collection. The result of a subtraction is called a difference.

Subtraction is written using the minus sign "âˆ’" between the terms; that is, in infix notation. The result is expressed with an equals sign.

Example: 6 - 2 = 4

Here are subtraction problems to understand subtraction operation.

  • Peter has 3 appels and Maria took 1 apple from Peter, how many apples Peter now have? (The Answer is 2).
  • On the road there were 4 red cars 3 yellow cars and 2 blue cars went from the road, how many cars are on the road? (The Answer is 7)
